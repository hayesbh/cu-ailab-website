Program Overview

The Master of Science in Artificial Intelligence at CU Boulder offers a rigorous, interdisciplinary curriculum designed to prepare students for leadership roles in the rapidly evolving field of AI. Our program combines theoretical foundations with practical application, leveraging the cutting-edge research of the CAIR Group.

Students will engage in deep learning, natural language processing, computer vision, and robotics, working alongside world-class faculty. The program culminates in a capstone project or thesis that addresses real-world challenges, ensuring graduates are industry-ready.

Core & Elective Courses

Core Requirements

CSCI 5622 arrow_forward
Machine Learning

Supervised learning, unsupervised learning, reinforcement learning, and learning theory.

CSCI 5454 arrow_forward
Design & Analysis of Algorithms

Advanced topics in algorithm design, including graph algorithms, approximation algorithms, and randomized algorithms.

CSCI 5722 arrow_forward
Computer Vision

Image formation, feature detection, segmentation, and 3D reconstruction.

CSCI 5832 arrow_forward
Natural Language Processing

Fundamental techniques for processing, analyzing, and understanding human language.

Degree Requirements

1
Undergraduate Degree

Bachelor's degree in Computer Science, Mathematics, or a related field with a minimum GPA of 3.0.

2
Standardized Tests

GRE scores are currently waived for Fall 2024 applicants. TOEFL/IELTS required for international students.

3
Letters of Recommendation

Three letters of recommendation from academic or professional references familiar with your potential for graduate study.
